About Smart City

A Smart City is an urban area that uses digital technology and data-driven insights to improve its residents' quality of life, optimize city operations, and promote environmental sustainability. By integrating the Artificial Intelligence (AI), and advanced communication networks like 5G, smart cities "sense" their environment to make more informed, real-time decisions.

For an individual person, a Smart City is designed to act like an invisible assistant that removes the "friction" of urban living. While the technical side is about sensors and data, the human side is about three things: Time, Health, and Agency.

Uzbekistan is rapidly implementing "Smart City" technologies under its Digital Uzbekistan - 2030 strategy. The country is moving beyond simple digital services to building entire purpose-built urban centers from the ground up, with a primary focus on the Tashkent region.

Benefits

Benefits of Smart City

Smart city initiatives offer transformative benefits for the entire world by addressing critical global challenges such as climate change, rapid urbanization, and economic inequality. By 2026, the widespread adoption of these technologies is catalyzing a shift toward more sustainable, resilient, and inclusive societies.

For people living in these cities, the "Smart" label translates into tangible improvements in daily life. It’s less about the gadgets and more about giving people their time, health, and safety back.

Smart Cities provide a wide range of benefits that improve urban living by making cities more efficient, safe, and environmentally friendly. According to the Digital Uzbekistan – 2030 strategy, these benefits are central to the country's urban development goals.
